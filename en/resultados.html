Key Finding: Geography Can Reduce Emissions up to 90×

The same model trained in Iceland (geothermal) vs Poland (coal) produces a difference of 90× in carbon emissions. This means that datacenter location choice is the single most impactful factor on AI sustainability — more than model size or hardware efficiency. This conclusion has direct implications for datacenter location policies.

2.4Parameters–Emissions Correlation: Power Law

Statistical analysis of 30+ models reveals a power-law correlation between parameter count and CO₂ emissions (R²=0.89, p<0.001). Each doubling of parameters implies approximately a 1.6× increase in emissions. This relationship is non-linear: going from 7B to 70B parameters (~10×) implies approximately 16× more emissions. The correlation confirms that model scale is the most reliable predictor of environmental impact, although geography and hardware introduce significant variations.
