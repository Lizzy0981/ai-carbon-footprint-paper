Section 1 — Methodology

Carbon Footprint Measurement Methodology

Validated formula, hardware specifications, regional carbon factors, and empirical validation process

This section presents the methodological framework developed to quantify the carbon footprint of AI models. The methodology builds on Strubell et al. (2019) and Patterson et al. (2021), with refinements derived from estimation practice and validation against official data published by Google, Microsoft, and Meta AI.

1.1Main Calculation Formula
Core Model Equation
CO₂(kg) = N_GPUs × P_GPU(kW) × T_h × PUE × C_factor
N_GPUs
Number of GPUs used in training
P_GPU (kW)
Thermal design power per GPU (TDP)
T_h
Training time in hours
PUE
Power Usage Effectiveness of the datacenter
C_factor
Carbon intensity factor (kg CO₂/kWh) by geographic region

Methodological note: The formula calculates Scope 2 emissions (consumed electricity). It does not include Scope 3 hardware manufacturing emissions (Scope 1 embodied carbon). For a full lifecycle footprint, multiply the result by a factor of 1.4–2.0x depending on the hardware used and equipment lifespan.

1.3Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) by Datacenter Type

PUE measures overall datacenter efficiency: a value of 1.0 would be perfect (all energy goes to computing). The global average is ~1.58, but the most efficient hyperscalers achieve values of 1.1–1.2.
